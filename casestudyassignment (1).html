01

Product Understanding

Problem · Audience · Why not do it manually

To land one offer today you often need 100 to 200 applications. Done properly, each one costs 3 to 5 minutes. That's hundreds of hours.

The real trap is that the two things that actually get interviews, applying in volume and applying personally, pull against each other. By hand you get one or the other, never both. So people burn out, start firing off generic resumes, and watch their response rate quietly collapse.

Who it's for. The core user is the high-volume applicant: recent grads and early-career switchers, who have the most applications to send and the least leverage. Then mid-career switchers and passive seekers who want to stay on the market without it eating their week. It's deliberately not for senior execs, who hire through their network and apply in small numbers.

Why choose it over applying manually. It breaks the volume-versus-personalization tradeoff. AI handles the tailoring at scale, automation clears the busywork, and the dashboard gives you the one thing manual applying never does: a clear read on what's actually working.

02

MVP Planning

Version 1 in two weeks · build vs postpone

Two weeks isn't enough to build everything, and trying to would be the wrong instinct. V1's job is one thing: a core loop people trust. Track, tailor, measure.

Build first

  • Application tracker + status pipeline
    The backbone. Everyone needs it, pure logic, zero AI risk.
  • Multiple resumes + AI tailoring per JD
    The core AI value, and it's contained: text in, text out, user reviews first.
  • Basic analytics
    Sent, response rate, interview rate, pending. Almost free once tracking exists.
  • Assisted job add
    Paste a JD or URL instead of auto-discovering everything on day one.

Postpone

  • Auto job discovery from LinkedIn
    Anti-bot walls, ToS exposure, breaks on every layout change.
  • Auto-apply on the user's behalf
    The most dangerous feature to ship early. Every form differs, failures are silent, one bad submission loses trust for good.
  • Auto-sending recruiter outreach
    Deliverability, spam law, email-finding accuracy. Semi-automate later with approve-before-send.

Everything postponed is legally risky, technically fragile, or catastrophic when it misfires. You earn the right to automate the scary parts by first being reliable at the boring ones.

04

AI Integration

Where it belongs, and where it doesn't

AI handles the fuzzy, language-shaped judgment calls. Plain code handles anything with one correct answer. Knowing where not to use AI is as much a design decision as knowing where to use it.

Use AI here

  • Resume tailoring per JD
    Rewriting, reordering, aligning keywords.
  • Drafting recruiter outreach
    Personalized notes that still read human.
  • Parsing a JD
    Pulling out requirements, checking eligibility.
  • Ghost Job Detector
    Reading language and pattern signals in a listing.

Don't use AI here

  • Dashboard + analytics math
    It's arithmetic. An LLM here is slower, pricier, and a hallucination waiting to happen.
  • Deduplication + date handling
    Deterministic logic does it perfectly.
  • Reminders + email scheduling
    Plumbing, not judgment.
  • Status tracking + storage
    One correct answer, so no model needed.
05

Challenges

The five biggest, and how I'd handle them
01Discovery and scraping at scale
LinkedIn, Naukri, and Indeed run aggressive anti-bot defenses, and scraping breaks their terms and their layouts constantly. Start user-initiated: paste a URL, or a browser extension that reads the page the user is already on, which isn't scraping. Use official APIs where they exist and fail gracefully.
02Auto-apply reliability
Every form differs and failures are silent, so the user thinks they applied when they didn't. No full auto-apply in V1. Assisted autofill with a human confirm, and template the big ATS platforms first since Workday, Greenhouse, and Lever cover most postings. Log every attempt.
03Recruiter email accuracy and legality
Wrong addresses, spam flags, and GDPR exposure all live here. Verified email-finding with confidence scores, a human approve-before-send step, sending limits and warm-up, and one-click opt-out. No bulk blasting, ever.
04AI making things up on resumes
An LLM inventing experience the candidate doesn't have can cost them the job and their credibility. Constrain the model to facts already in the user's own resume, so it's retrieval-grounded rewriting rather than free generation. Show the diffs and require a review before anything is sent.
05Cost and latency at scale
Every tailor and parse is an LLM call, and across hundreds of applications and many users that becomes real money and a sluggish experience. Cache hard, use a small cheap model for parsing and save the strong model for final user-facing copy, batch background work, and call AI only on a user action.
06

One Feature Nobody Asked For

The thing that makes it stand out
Ghost Job Detector

Every rival helps you apply to more jobs. None protect your effort.

A trust layer that scores each listing for how real it actually is, before you spend a personalized application on it. It reads repost age, vague JDs with no hiring manager named, missing salary, the same role blasted across dozens of locations, and staffing-agency churn.

The output is dead simple: one flag, one reason.

Green · spend your best effort Amber · proceed with caution Red · likely not real

It inverts the whole category. Everyone else optimizes for quantity going out. This optimizes the quality of where your energy goes. It's the rare feature that's genuinely on the user's side.

Rejection Autopsy →

Uses your own outcome data to spot the pattern behind your rejections and ghostings, then hands you one concrete fix. Turns a sender into a coach.

Warm-Intro Finder →

Surfaces 2nd-degree connections at a target company and drafts the ask, so a cold application quietly becomes a warm one.

07

Tech Stack

What I'd build it on, and why
Frontend
React + Tailwind. The dashboard is the product surface, so a fast component-driven UI matters most here.
Backend
FastAPI on Python. Async, quick to build, and shares a language with the whole AI and data layer.
Data
Postgres for structured data, pgvector or Chroma for resume and JD embeddings. Powers retrieval-grounded tailoring.
AI Layer
An LLM API for tailoring, outreach, and JD parsing, plus embeddings for matching. A RAG pattern grounds every rewrite in the user's own resume so the model can't fabricate.
Automation
A browser extension to capture jobs, Playwright for assisted autofill. On the major ATS platforms first.
Infra
A job queue (Celery or RQ) for background parsing and batch tailoring, with caching. Keeps cost and latency in check.
